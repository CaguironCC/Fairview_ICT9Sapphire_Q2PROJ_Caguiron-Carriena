Lumpiang Sariwa Recipe

Lumpiang Sariwa is a Filipino fresh spring roll made with a mix of sautéed vegetables, sometimes with meat or shrimp, wrapped in a soft, crepe-like wrapper. It is typically served with a savory garlic-peanut sauce and is a popular dish for special occasions or as a light snack. Refreshing, healthy, and flavorful, it's a favorite in Filipino cuisine.

Source

Ingredients

  • 6 to 8 pieces Romaine lettuce
  • 1/2 cup crushed peanuts
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • 10 ounces extra firm tofu (fried and cubed)
  • 1/2 cup small shrimp (shell removed)
  • 1 piece Knorr Shrimp Cube
  • 1/2 head cabbage (chopped)
  • 3/4 cup green beans (sliced crosswise)
  • 1 piece medium carrot (julienne)
  • 1 cup sweet potato (cubed)
  • 1 piece white onion (chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 pieces eggs
  • 2 tablespoons canola oil
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup cooking oil or cooking oil spray
  • 6 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon garlic (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch

Instructions

  1. Prepare the filling: Heat oil in a pan. Sauté onion and garlic until softened. Add sweet potato, pour in water, and let it boil. Stir in the Knorr Shrimp Cube, cover, and cook for 5 minutes. Add tofu and shrimp, stir-fry for 1 minute. Add cabbage, green beans, and carrot, and stir-fry for 3 minutes. Season with fish sauce and black pepper. Set aside.
  2. Make the wrapper: In a large bowl, whisk eggs, canola oil, and water. Slowly add flour and whisk until smooth. Heat a non-stick pan, lightly grease it with oil, and pour 7-8 tablespoons of the mixture into the pan, swirling to spread. Cook for 1 minute or until the wrapper detaches from the pan. Repeat until all the mixture is used.
  3. Prepare the sauce: In a saucepan, boil water, soy sauce, brown sugar, and garlic. Stir, then dissolve cornstarch in water and add to the sauce. Stir continuously until it thickens.
  4. Assemble the Lumpia: Lay a wrapper flat on a plate, place a lettuce leaf in the center, and top with the filling. Fold the lower part of the wrapper over the filling, then fold the sides. Arrange the lumpia on a plate, pour over the sauce, and sprinkle with crushed peanuts and chopped parsley.
  5. Serve: Enjoy your fresh Lumpiang Sariwa!
