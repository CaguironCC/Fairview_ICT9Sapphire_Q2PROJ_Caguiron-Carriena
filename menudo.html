Pork Giniling Recipe

Pork Menudo is a beloved Filipino comfort food, often served during fiestas and gatherings. It features tender pork simmered in a savory tomato sauce, seasoned with soy sauce and lemon, and complemented with liver, potatoes, carrots, and hotdogs. Each region in the Philippines has its own variation, making it a versatile and comforting dish.

Source

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork, cut into cubes
  • 1/4 lb pig liver, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup diced potatoes
  • 1 large carrot, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • 3 cloves garlic, peeled and minced
  • 1 onion, peeled and finely chopped
  • 3/4 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 cup water
  • 4 pieces hotdogs, sliced
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Marinate the pork in soy sauce and lemon for at least 30 minutes.
  2. In a pan, saut√© garlic and onion until softened and aromatic.
  3. Add the marinated pork and cook, stirring occasionally, until lightly browned.
  4. Add the tomato sauce, water, and bay leaves. Stir to combine and bring to a simmer. Lower heat and let it cook until the pork is tender, about 25-30 minutes.
  5. Add the liver and hotdogs, and cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  6. Add the potatoes, carrots, and season with salt and pepper to taste. Continue to simmer until the vegetables are tender, about 10 minutes.
  7. Serve hot, ideally with steamed rice, and enjoy your flavorful Pork Menudo!
