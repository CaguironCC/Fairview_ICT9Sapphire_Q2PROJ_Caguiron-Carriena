Pork Adobo Recipe

Pork Adobo is a savory Filipino dish made by marinating pork in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and bay leaves. It is cooked slowly to achieve a tender and flavorful result, and is usually paired with steamed rice for the ultimate comfort food experience.

Source

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly
  • 2 tablespoons garlic (crushed)
  • 5 dried bay leaves
  • 4 tablespoons vinegar
  • Â½ cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon peppercorn
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Marinate: Combine the pork belly with soy sauce and garlic in a bowl. Let it sit for at least 1 hour for the flavors to meld.
  2. Cook: In a pot, add the marinated pork and heat for a few minutes. Then, pour in the remaining marinade, water, peppercorns, and bay leaves. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer for 40 minutes to 1 hour until the pork is tender.
  3. Add vinegar: Stir in the vinegar and simmer for an additional 12-15 minutes. Adjust seasoning with salt to your taste.
  4. Serve: Serve the Pork Adobo hot with a side of steamed rice and enjoy!
